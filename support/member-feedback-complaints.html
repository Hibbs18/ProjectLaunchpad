Member Feedback & Complaints Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025

Your Voice Matters
At Project Launchpad, we believe that member feedback—both positive and constructive—is essential to our continuous improvement and success. This policy outlines how we collect, handle, and respond to feedback and complaints, ensuring every member voice is heard, valued, and acted upon with fairness and transparency.

1. Purpose & Principles

1.1 Why This Policy Exists

This policy ensures that:

  • Members have clear, accessible channels for providing feedback
  • Complaints are handled fairly, promptly, and transparently
  • We continuously improve based on member input
  • All feedback is treated with respect and confidentiality
  • Members understand their rights and our commitments

1.2 Core Principles

  • Accessibility: Multiple easy-to-use feedback channels
  • Responsiveness: Timely acknowledgment and resolution
  • Fairness: Impartial investigation and decision-making
  • Transparency: Clear processes and communication
  • Confidentiality: Privacy protection throughout the process
  • Continuous Improvement: Learning from every interaction

2. Types of Feedback

2.1 General Feedback

What it includes:

  • Suggestions for platform improvements
  • Ideas for new features or resources
  • Positive experiences you'd like to share
  • General observations about our services

2.2 Service Issues

What it includes:

  • Technical problems or bugs
  • Access or login difficulties
  • Questions about platform features
  • Resource availability concerns

2.3 Formal Complaints

What it includes:

  • Concerns about service quality or delivery
  • Billing or payment disputes
  • Privacy or data security concerns
  • Code of Conduct violations
  • Disagreements with PLP decisions or policies

3.3 Response Time Commitments

  • Acknowledgment: Within 2 business days (all channels)
  • Initial response: Within 5 business days
  • Standard complaints: 5-10 business days for resolution
  • Appeals: 10 business days for decision
  • Complex issues: May require additional time; we'll keep you updated

4. Complaint Process

4.1 Step 1: Submit Your Complaint

What to include:

  • Your name and contact information
  • Member organization name (if applicable)
  • Detailed description of the issue
  • Relevant dates, times, and documentation
  • What resolution you're seeking
  • Any previous attempts to resolve the issue

4.2 Step 2: Acknowledgment

Within 2 business days, you'll receive:

  • Confirmation we've received your complaint
  • A unique reference number for tracking
  • Name of the person handling your case
  • Expected timeline for resolution
  • Next steps in the process

4.3 Step 3: Investigation

We will:

  • Review all relevant information and documentation
  • Speak with involved parties (with your consent)
  • Gather additional context as needed
  • Maintain confidentiality throughout
  • Keep you updated on progress

4.4 Step 4: Resolution

You'll receive a written response that includes:

  • Summary of the investigation findings
  • Our decision and reasoning
  • Actions we'll take (if applicable)
  • Timeline for implementation
  • Your right to appeal

4.5 Step 5: Follow-Up

After resolution, we:

  • Check in to ensure you're satisfied
  • Implement any agreed-upon changes
  • Document lessons learned
  • Update policies/procedures if needed

5. Investigation Process

5.1 Our Approach

All complaints are investigated:

  • Impartially: Without bias or preconceptions
  • Thoroughly: Examining all relevant evidence
  • Confidentially: Protecting privacy of all parties
  • Fairly: Giving all parties opportunity to be heard
  • Promptly: Within stated timelines

5.2 What We Consider

  • All information provided by the complainant
  • Relevant policies, procedures, and agreements
  • Input from involved staff or members
  • Historical context and precedents
  • Industry best practices and standards

5.3 Possible Outcomes

  • Complaint upheld: We agree with your concern and will take action
  • Partially upheld: Some aspects valid, others not
  • Not upheld: After investigation, we cannot support the complaint
  • Resolved informally: Issue addressed through dialogue
  • Referred elsewhere: Better handled by another process/body

6. Appeals Process

6.1 When You Can Appeal

You may appeal if you believe:

  • The investigation was incomplete or unfair
  • New evidence has emerged
  • The decision was based on incorrect information
  • Proper procedures weren't followed
  • The resolution is inadequate

6.2 How to Appeal

Submit your appeal to: complaints@projectlaunchpad.ca

Include:

  • Original complaint reference number
  • Reasons for appealing
  • Any new evidence or information
  • What outcome you're seeking

Timeline: Appeals must be submitted within 30 days of receiving the original decision.

6.3 Appeals Review

Your appeal will be reviewed by:

  • A senior leader not involved in the original decision
  • Someone with appropriate expertise and authority
  • An impartial party who will consider all perspectives

Decision timeline: 10 business days from receipt of appeal

6.4 Appeal Outcomes

  • Appeal upheld: Original decision overturned or modified
  • Appeal denied: Original decision stands
  • Further investigation: Additional review needed

The appeals decision is final within Project Launchpad's internal process.

7. External Escalation

7.1 When Internal Process is Exhausted

If you've completed our internal complaint and appeal process and remain dissatisfied, you may escalate to:

Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada

For privacy-related complaints:

  • Website: www.priv.gc.ca
  • Toll-free: 1-800-282-1376
  • Email: info@priv.gc.ca

Better Business Bureau

For general business complaints:

  • Website: www.bbb.org
  • Search for Project Launchpad, Harmonia Philanthropy, or Telos One

Ontario Ministry of Public and Business Service Delivery

For consumer protection issues:

  • Website: www.ontario.ca/consumers
  • Toll-free: 1-800-889-9768

Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre

For fraud-related concerns:

  • Website: www.antifraudcentre-centreantifraude.ca
  • Toll-free: 1-888-495-8501

7.2 Legal Action

You retain the right to pursue legal remedies through courts if you believe:

  • Our policies violate your legal rights
  • You've suffered damages due to our actions
  • Contract terms have been breached
  • Other legal grounds exist

See our Terms of Service Section 13 (Dispute Resolution) for governing law and jurisdiction.

7.3 Our Cooperation

PLP commits to:

  • Cooperating fully with legitimate external investigations
  • Providing requested documentation in timely manner
  • Respecting decisions of external bodies
  • Implementing required corrective action
  • Maintaining no retaliation for external escalation

8. Learning & Improvement

8.1 Feedback Integration

How We Use Feedback:

  • Quarterly review of all feedback and complaints
  • Identification of patterns and systemic issues
  • Prioritization of improvements in development roadmap
  • Policy updates based on recurring themes
  • Training programs addressing common concerns

8.2 Transparency

Annual summary of complaint statistics (anonymized):

  • Published in our Impact Report
  • Includes: total complaints, categories, resolution rates, average response times
  • Trends and improvements implemented

8.3 Member Advisory Input

Quarterly Member Feedback Sessions:

  • Open forums for discussing platform improvements
  • Direct dialogue with PLP leadership
  • Opportunity to shape future direction
  • Recognition of members who contribute valuable feedback

How to Participate:

  • Watch for invitations via email
  • Volunteer by emailing feedback@projectlaunchpad.ca
  • Both Lite and Lift-Off members welcome

9. Special Circumstances

9.1 Anonymous Feedback

We Accept Anonymous:

  • General suggestions and ideas
  • Platform bug reports
  • Content error notifications

We Cannot Accept Anonymous:

  • Formal complaints requiring investigation
  • Complaints about specific individuals
  • Issues requiring follow-up communication

9.2 Third-Party Complaints

If Someone Else Files a Complaint About You:

You have the right to:

  • Be informed that a complaint has been filed
  • Understand the nature of allegations
  • Provide your perspective fairly
  • Have investigation conducted fairly
  • Appeal any adverse decisions

Process:

  • We notify you within 2 business days of receiving complaint
  • You have 5 business days to respond
  • Investigation considers all perspectives
  • Decisions based on evidence, not allegations alone

9.3 Complaints Involving Multiple Organizations

For disputes between member organizations:

  • PLP can facilitate dialogue and mediation
  • We cannot force specific outcomes or take sides
  • Focus on ensuring Code of Conduct compliance
  • May involve neutral third-party mediators
  • Resolution focuses on future behavior, not judging past

Our Commitment

Every piece of feedback—whether a suggestion, concern, or complaint—helps us serve Canada's smaller charities better. We're committed to listening, learning, and continuously improving based on your input.

Your willingness to share both positive experiences and areas for improvement demonstrates your investment in Project Launchpad's success. We don't just welcome your feedback; we depend on it to fulfill our mission.
