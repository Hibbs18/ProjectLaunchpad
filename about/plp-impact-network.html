Founding Members

The visionary founding organizations who co-created and helped shape Project Launchpad from day one.

ALSO Ottawa

Alternative Learning Styles and Outlooks (ALSO) has been serving Ottawa adults and families since 1981. We take pride in being a small program that achieves great things. We offer support to adults who may not feel they fit into more mainstream programs by offering a flexible, person-centred approach to learning and skill development.

Bereaved Families of Ontario - Ottawa

BFO is Ottawa’s leading non-profit charity offering loss-specific peer support, we help families and individuals grappling with grief and bereavement. BFO Ottawa has helped over 3,000 people in Ottawa annually through its 300 support programs, all led by trained volunteer facilitators.


OrKidstra

OrKidstra is an Ottawa-based charitable social development program that empowers kids from equity-deserving communities by teaching life skills through singing and playing music together. OrKidstra began in 2007 with 27 children; today it has grown to include over 750 children and youth from 62 cultural and linguistic backgrounds.

Rocena Kidney Foundation

Founded to serve the Black and African-Caribbean community in Ottawa, the Rocena Kidney Foundation is dedicated to improving kidney health and quality of life for individuals affected by kidney disease.The Foundation delivers quarterly awareness sessions led by health professionals, bi-monthly peer support groups, and culturally competent outreach programs.

Serenity Renewal For Families

Serenity Renewal for Families is dedicated to providing hope and support to individuals and families affected by addiction and related issues. Our mission is to foster happier, healthier lives through short-term counselling, adult educational workshops, and programs in a safe environment. Serenity Renewal has impacted over 100,000 individuals in Ottawa and surrounding areas, serving more than 2,000 people annually.

Watersheds Canada

Founded in 2002, Watersheds Canada is federally incorporated non-profit and registered charity with a mandate to help local organizations, volunteers and waterfront property owners deliver freshwater stewardship and education programs in their community. We have saved over 40km of degraded shorelines, completed 55 fish habitat projects on 35 water bodies, and planted over 155,000 native plants.

How the Network Works

Every partner in our network is vetted for quality, reliability, and alignment with our mission. We don't just create a directory—we facilitate meaningful connections that drive results.

Our Vetting Process

We take partner selection seriously. Every organization or individual in our network must demonstrate:

Proven experience working with smaller charities or nonprofits

Commitment to accessible pricing and flexible engagement models

Strong references and track record of quality work

Alignment with our values of transparency and impact

Understanding of the unique challenges facing smaller organizations

Become a Network Partner

Are you a consultant, service provider, or organization interested in supporting smaller Canadian charities? We're always looking to expand our network with partners who share our values and commitment to accessible, high-quality support.

Partner Benefits Include:

Access to a community of mission-driven organizations

Visibility to charities actively seeking your services
